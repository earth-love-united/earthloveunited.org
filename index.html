Climate Science Primer
Where Does Carbon Go?
Carbon moves through the atmosphere, oceans, vegetation, soils, and human systems. The problem is not that carbon moves; the problem is that human activity has pushed the exchange out of balance.

Climate Science Primer
The Annual Imbalance
Current global flows are uneven: natural sinks absorb a large share of human emissions, but not all of them. The remaining gap, roughly 20 Gt CO₂ per year in this site model, accumulates in the atmosphere and ocean.

The exact number changes with inventories and methods. The durable lesson is simpler: climate stability depends on reducing fossil emissions while protecting and restoring the sinks that still carry part of the burden.
Climate Science Primer
Not All Green Is Equal
A forest, a mangrove, a grassland, and a plantation can all appear green from orbit. Their carbon stocks, recovery times, water relationships, and biodiversity value are very different.
Carbon Density (tC/ha)
Restoration is not a generic tree count. It is a place-based decision about carbon, water, biodiversity, land tenure, and community benefit.
